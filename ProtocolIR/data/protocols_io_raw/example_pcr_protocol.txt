PCR Master Mix Plate Setup Protocol
Source: protocols.io (example)

GOAL:
Prepare a 96-well PCR plate with DNA templates and PCR master mix for thermal cycling.

MATERIALS REQUIRED:
- DNA template samples (96 samples in tube rack)
- PCR master mix (contains primers, Taq polymerase, dNTPs, buffer)
- 96-well PCR plate (Biorad or equivalent)
- P20 and P300 single-channel pipettes
- 20 µL and 300 µL tip racks

PROCEDURE:

STEP 1: Preparation
Transfer DNA template samples from the tube rack to the PCR plate.
Each sample tube contains approximately 10 µL of DNA template (approximately 100-500 ng total).

STEP 2: DNA Template Distribution
Using a P20 pipette:
a) Pick up a fresh 20 µL tip
b) Aspirate 10 µL from tube A1 of the template rack
c) Dispense into well A1 of the PCR plate
d) Discard the tip
e) Repeat for all 96 samples

STEP 3: PCR Master Mix Addition
Using a P300 pipette:
a) Pick up a fresh 300 µL tip
b) Aspirate 40 µL of PCR master mix from the master mix tube
c) Dispense into each well of the PCR plate (wells A1-H12)
d) After dispensing into each well, gently mix by pipetting up and down 3 times with 30 µL volume
e) Change to a fresh tip every 24 wells to prevent contamination

STEP 4: Mixing
Gently mix the contents of each well by pipetting up and down 3-5 times with 30 µL volume.
Do NOT vortex as this may cause aerosol formation.

STEP 5: Plate Sealing
Seal the PCR plate with a clear adhesive seal.
Press firmly on all edges to ensure a good seal.

STEP 6: Final Check
Visually inspect the plate for any spilled liquid or contamination.
Keep the sealed plate on ice until thermal cycling.

NOTES:
- All pipetting should be performed carefully to avoid creating aerosols
- Master mix should be kept on ice until use
- If any sample is accidentally spilled, the well should be refilled with fresh template
- Thermal cycling should begin within 2 hours of plate preparation
